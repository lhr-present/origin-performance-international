01 // About

From National Team
Athlete to Elite Coach

Emre Cikinci

Emre Cikinci is a former elite international rower who represented the Turkish National Rowing Team for over a decade, accumulating more than 200 national and international medals across his competitive career. His firsthand experience at the highest levels of the sport — the technical demands, the competitive pressure, and the physical and mental discipline required — forms the foundation of his coaching approach.

Now based in South Florida, Emre coaches at the North Palm Beach Rowing Club (NPBRC), where he works with youth and junior athletes across sculling and sweep disciplines. Under his guidance, NPBRC athletes have become consistent podium finishers at the Florida State Rowing Association Championships and USRowing Southeast Youth Championships, with multiple athletes qualifying for and medalling at the USRowing Youth National Championships.

In 2024, his athletes earned a spot on the US National Team for the CanAmex competition, where they won Gold in the Women's Youth 4X — a landmark achievement. Beyond competition results, Emre takes pride in developing athletes who go on to earn Division I rowing scholarships at universities across the United States, including the University of Tennessee.

Through Oarigin Performance International, Emre brings his elite background and coaching expertise to athletes worldwide — individually and in groups, in-person and online.

04 // Philosophy
“Every athlete has a ceiling they cannot see yet. My job is to help them break through it — technically, physically, and mentally.”
◎

Athlete-First Development

Every program is built around the individual — their physiology, their timeline, their goals. There is no universal template. Understanding what makes each athlete tick is where coaching starts.

◈

Technical Mastery

Rowing is a technique sport before it is a fitness sport. Clean, efficient movement built on solid fundamentals is the non-negotiable foundation. Power built on poor mechanics is wasted potential.

◇

Long-Term Thinking

Results matter — but so does what comes after. Emre's athletes graduate to D1 programs, national teams, and lifelong relationships with the sport. Sustainable development over short-term gains, every time.
